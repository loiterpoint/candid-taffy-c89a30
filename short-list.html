The Short List:
five things worth buying.

One pick per category — the thing we'd buy today if we were spending our own money. Each one gets a reason to buy it and an honest reason you might not. If you want the full evidence, every pick links to its review.

01 · Drones
DJI Mini 5 Pro
$759
Sub-250g · 1-inch sensor · LiDAR obstacle sensing
Why

The most complete sub-250g drone made: a true 1-inch sensor, LiDAR that lets it fly and return home after sunset, and class-leading tested endurance.

Why not

The 249.9 g badge doesn't survive a scale — production units weigh 252–253 g, and the FAA allows no tolerance. Budget five dollars and five minutes to register it.

02 · Home
Roborock S8 Pro Ultra
$1,099
Self-empty · Self-clean mop · LiDAR nav
Why

Genuinely hands-free. The dock empties the bin, refills the mop water and washes the pads — the only one that actually earns the "set and forget" claim.

Why not

It's a grand. If you have pets or kids, the Roomba j7+ at $549 wins on obstacle avoidance and is the smarter buy for half the money.

03 · Audio
Sony WH-CH720N
$149
192g · ANC · Multipoint
Why

Light at 192 g, real ANC, and a battery that independent labs measured at 40+ hours with ANC on — beating its own spec. That's rare enough to be worth noting.

Why not

The Anker Q45 at $79 measured near 56 hours and closes more of the gap than the price difference suggests. Buy the Sony for comfort, not for a spec win.

04 · Automotive
Vantrue E2 Lite
$149
2.5K front · 1080p rear · Starvis 2
Why

Starvis 2 sensors front and rear at $149, and no subscription for features that should be built in — which rules out most of the competition on principle.

Why not

If you only ever park on the street and want 4K forensic detail on plates, you'll want to spend up. For everyone else this is the stopping point.

05 · Gaming audio
Turtle Beach Stealth 600 Gen 3
Under $100
Wireless · 80-hour rated battery
Why

One of the only products we track whose battery rating a lab found conservative — 80 hours claimed, 83h 42m measured to cutoff. Everything else on this page fell short of its number.

Why not

Our wired pick does the same job for less and never needs charging. Wireless is a convenience purchase here, not a performance one.
