What actually matters in a dorm room

Three constraints separate dorm gear from ordinary gear. Sound is shared, so cancel it or contain it: ANC earbuds and a wireless headset keep your audio yours and the room quiet, which does more for roommate peace than any negotiation. Space is tight, so footprint is a spec: a 75% keyboard, a stick instead of a box, and a bank instead of a second charger all buy back desk and outlet room you don't have. Power is scarce, so plan for the outlet strip: assume one shared strip and buy things that charge fast, sip battery, or run off USB. Campus Wi-Fi is usually handled for you, so a mesh system is one thing you can safely leave off the packing list.

How to shop the dorm shelf

Two rules keep back-to-school spending from turning into buyer's remorse. First, buy on the evidence, not the box: at these prices the loudest listings lean on numbers that don't survive independent testing — inflated battery ratings, "virtual surround," peak-lumen claims — so we weight measured results and long-term owner reports over marketing. Second, watch the street price, not the list price. Back-to-school and Prime-style events discount these categories hard, which is exactly why every figure here is approximate; a $99 headset routinely sells for $70 and a $50 stick dips to $35.
