⚡ Nerd Box: How We Verify a "Deal"

Retail "was" prices are the most gamed number in commerce — anchor a fake $499, "discount" to the everyday $299, and the brain's loss-aversion circuitry does the rest. So we don't take was-prices at face value. Before a deal makes this page, we cross-check the claimed discount against independent price-history tracking and published deal coverage: what has this product actually sold for over the past 90 days?

A "Lowest Tracked Price" badge means price-history tracking shows the product has never sold for less. No badge means it's a genuine discount, but the product has hit this price (or close) before — still a fine time to buy, just not a historic one. And when a deal is mediocre, we say so in the card (see the Osmo Action 5 Pro above). That's the whole system.

The Tricks We Refuse To Use

Deal pages are persuasion machines, so here's ours with the hood open. What you'll find here: real deadlines, real price history, real savings math. What you will never find here: countdown timers (they're theater — the clock resets), "only 3 left!" stock claims (we don't have Amazon's inventory data, and neither do most sites that show them), inflated "was" prices (the oldest trick in retail), or fake editor's awards. If a page needs to lie to sell you something, the deal was never good. Ours don't need to.
