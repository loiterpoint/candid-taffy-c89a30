How We Evaluate Dehumidifiers

Loiter Point does not operate a test lab. Instead, we build our rankings by cross-referencing published independent testing — the kind that puts every unit in the same sealed room and times how long it takes to drop relative humidity from 90% down to 40% — against verified owner reports gathered from retailer reviews. When a single number appears below, we label it as either the manufacturer's rated figure or an "est. real-world / reported" figure drawn from that published testing. We weight four things: moisture-removal speed under controlled conditions, measured power draw, noise output at the highest fan speed, and long-run reliability as reported by owners over months of use. Where the evidence is thin or the built-in hygrometer is known to drift, we say so rather than pretend to certainty. Prices shift constantly, so treat the figures here as recent street prices and check the live listing before you buy.

⚡ Nerd box: why "pints per day" lies to you

A dehumidifier's pint rating is measured under fixed lab conditions — historically a warm, very humid room. Run the same unit in a cool 60°F basement and its real output can fall by a third or more, because cold coils condense less water and are more prone to frosting. That's why low-temperature operation (which the Frigidaires above advertise) matters far more than a big headline pint number if your problem space is a chilly basement. It's also why independent 90%-to-40% timed tests, run on every unit in the same room, are a better guide than the sticker: they compare machines on equal footing. When you shop, match the pint rating to your space and temperature, not to the biggest number on the box. And if you want truly hands-off operation, buy the drain hose (or a pump model) and forget the tank entirely.
