Air purifier marketing is built on one dishonest number: the room-size claim. A box that says "cleans 3,235 sq ft" is quoting a single air change per hour — nowhere near enough to actually control smoke or allergens. Serious guidance targets roughly 4–5 air changes per hour (ACH), which cuts that claim to a quarter or a fifth of the printed figure. So we ignore the square-footage headline and rank on CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) — an AHAM-verified measure of how many cubic feet of clean air the unit actually delivers per minute for smoke, dust and pollen.

Every pick below uses genuine mechanical True HEPA filtration (or Blueair's HEPASilent hybrid), avoids ozone-generating "ionizer-only" gimmicks, and has a long enough track record in published independent testing and owner reports to trust. // NERD BOX — CADR & the ACH math

Why the box's square-footage lies to you

CADR is measured in CFM (cubic feet per minute) of particle-free air delivered. To convert CADR into an honest room size at a target air-change rate, the rule of thumb is: room area (sq ft) ≈ CADR × 1.55 ÷ ACH (assuming 8 ft ceilings). Manufacturers quote coverage at 1 ACH — one full room-volume of clean air per hour — which barely touches active pollution.

For allergy and smoke control, the EPA-adjacent guidance and independent reviewers target 4.8 ACH. Plug in the Coway's ~240 CADR: 240 × 1.55 ÷ 4.8 ≈ 78 sq ft per air change of headroom above the room — practically, ~160–200 sq ft of real coverage, not the 361 on the box. Always divide the printed room claim by ~4–5 to get the room it will actually keep clean. And ignore "ionizer" or "plasma" CADR-free claims entirely: if a mode has no CADR number, it isn't moving measurable clean air.

How We Evaluate Air Purifiers

Loiter Point does not run a lab and does not claim to. Our rankings synthesize published independent testing (AHAM-verified CADR data, plus hands-on measurements from established reviewers) and aggregated verified-owner reports on noise, filter longevity and real-world dust/smoke performance. We prioritize, in order: verified CADR, honest air-change coverage math, filtration type (mechanical True HEPA over ozone-risk ionizers), long-term filter cost, and measured noise. Where public evidence is thin or conflicting — as it often is for exact dB and filter-life figures — we say so and label spec rows "reported" or "est. real-world" rather than implying we measured them ourselves.
