Sony WH-1000XM5 Review: Still the Smart Buy Now That the XM6 Is Here

The XM5 is no longer Sony's flagship — the WH-1000XM6 took that title in 2025. But a funny thing happened on the way to obsolescence: the XM5 got cheaper, kept nearly all of its strengths, and turned into one of the best-value pairs of premium noise-cancelling headphones you can buy. Here is what the published measurements and owner reports actually say.

The Short Version

  • Buy the Sony WH-1000XM5 if you want class-leading ANC and comfort without paying flagship money. It has dropped as low as $198 in 2026 versus a $399.99 list — roughly half price for about 90% of the newest model's performance.
  • Step up to the Sony WH-1000XM6 only if you specifically want the folding design, the newer 12-mic ANC system, longer battery, and Bluetooth LE Audio / Auracast — and you're fine paying ~$449 for incremental gains.
  • Save more with the Sony WH-1000XM4 if you want most of the experience for the lowest price and don't mind losing simultaneous LDAC + multipoint.
  • Loiter Point doesn't run a lab. Every figure below is sourced from published independent testing (SoundGuys, RTINGS-style measurements) or verified owner reports, and labeled as such.
Grab your pick: XM5 — ~$198–$399 · XM6 — ~$449 · XM4 — ~$248

How We Evaluate Headphones

Loiter Point does not operate an anechoic chamber or a head-and-torso measurement rig, and we're not going to pretend otherwise. What we do is synthesize the published measurements from independent labs that do run that equipment — battery-drain tests, isolation sweeps, frequency-response plots — and cross-check them against the pattern of verified owner reports for durability and real-world quirks. When the independent sources disagree, we say so rather than papering over it. Every performance number in this review is attributed to its source and framed as a measurement or a report, never as something we tested ourselves.

The XM5 pairs a lightweight, genuinely all-day-comfortable fit with noise cancellation that independent reviewers consistently rate among the best available. Passive isolation is strong on its own, and the active system layers on top of it to knock down the low-frequency drone of planes and trains especially well. Call quality from the beamforming mic array is a standout — owner reports repeatedly single it out as clearer than most rivals in wind and background chatter.

Two caveats keep this from being a blind recommendation. First, the XM5 does not fold flat — the swivel-only hinge means a larger carrying case, which matters if you're packing light. Second, that same hinge is the part owners most often flag for durability concerns over long ownership; the XM6 specifically added visible metal reinforcement to address it. Treat the headband hinge with a little care and it's a non-issue for most people, but it's worth knowing before you buy.

Nerd box: what "90% of the XM6" actually means

The headline gaps between the XM5 and the newer XM6 are real but narrow. Independent battery testing put the XM6 at roughly 37h 14m with ANC on versus the XM5's ~31h 53m — about five extra hours. The XM6 moves to a faster QN3 processor and a 12-microphone array (up from 8), which reviewers describe as a modest, not dramatic, ANC improvement. It also folds and adds Bluetooth LE Audio / Auracast. None of that is nothing — but at a street price gap that can approach $250 when the XM5 is discounted, "90% of the performance for roughly half the money" is a fair summary of where the XM5 sits in 2026.

Bottom Line

The arrival of the WH-1000XM6 didn't make the XM5 worse — it made it a bargain. You get noise cancellation, comfort, and call quality that independent reviewers still rank near the top of the category, and you increasingly get it for close to half of what the current flagship costs. Buy the XM5 if value is the goal, step up to the XM6 only if the specific upgrades matter to you, and grab the XM4 if you want to spend the least. Whichever you choose, watch the live price — this is a category where patience is regularly rewarded.
