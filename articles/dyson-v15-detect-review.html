Dyson V15 Detect Review: The Value Flagship That Refuses to Retire

By Loiter Point Staff · Updated July 2026

Where the V15 Detect Sits in 2026

Dyson has expanded its cordless range considerably since the V15 Detect arrived in March 2021. The current flagship is the Gen5detect (from $1,049.99), which Dyson positions above the V15 with more suction, a longer-running battery, a larger 0.1-micron HEPA filter and a push-button power control instead of a squeeze trigger. Above that sit the newer V16 and PencilVac. Crucially, the V15 Detect was not discontinued when those launched. It slid down into the value/mid-flagship slot, which is exactly why it is interesting: you get most of the flagship cleaning story at a meaningfully lower price. For context, the entry-level Dyson V8 costs roughly half as much with less power, and is the budget alternative for anyone who does not need this much vacuum.

Cleaning Performance: What the Labs Actually Measured

This is where the V15 earns its reputation. TechGearLab's independent testing found 99% pickup on hardwood (a 9.0/10 hardwood score) and strong carpet numbers: 94% low-pile pickup and 49% high-pile pickup, both above the category averages of 82.5% and 42.1% respectively. On pet hair, TechGearLab measured 88% pickup on low-pile and 60% on high-pile, again ahead of the 72.4% / 48.4% category averages.

Vacuum Wars, using its own scoring, reached the same conclusion from a different angle: an overall 4.77/5 versus a 2.53/5 category average, with a perfect carpet deep-clean score in their embedded-sand test. Their component ratings were Performance 4.51 (vs 2.98 avg), Hard Floor 3.70 (vs 1.91 avg), Carpet 4.56 (vs 3.7 avg), Features 4.24 (vs 1.85 avg) and Battery 4.31 (vs 2.4 avg). Two independent buyers, two testing methods, one verdict: this vacuum cleans.

The Suction Numbers, Honestly Labeled

Dyson rates the V15 Detect at 240 AW (air watts) of suction — that is a manufacturer specification, not an independently verified measurement. The figure a lab actually measured is different in both metric and meaning: TechGearLab measured 2,239 pascals of suction, versus 2,388 Pa for the category-leading Gen5detect. In other words, the V15 sits just below the current flagship on raw measured suction, which tracks with the price gap between them. Do not conflate the two numbers — AW and Pa measure different things — and be skeptical of any spec sheet that only quotes the flattering one.

Nerd Box

Air watts vs pascals, and why "230AW" figures float around

You will see the V15's suction quoted as air watts (AW), pascals (Pa) or kilopascals (kPa) depending on the source, and they are not interchangeable. Air watts is Dyson's preferred spec — it combines airflow and vacuum pressure into a single "cleaning power" figure, and Dyson states 240 AW for the V15 Detect. Pascals measures pressure alone; TechGearLab's bench measured 2,239 Pa. Both are legitimate, but they answer different questions, so a bigger AW number does not linearly map to a bigger Pa number. We are deliberately ignoring the unsourced "17.6 kPa," "124-inch sealed suction" and "230AW" figures that circulate in AI-generated summaries because none of them trace back to a primary source we could verify. Trust the manufacturer's clearly-labeled 240 AW spec and TechGearLab's measured 2,239 Pa — nothing in between.

The Two Real Weaknesses

No pretending here. First, the trigger must be held down continuously while cleaning. TechGearLab flagged this as a source of hand fatigue on long sessions, and it is one of the headline reasons Dyson moved the Gen5detect to a push-button. Related: at Max power the strong suction can make the head hard to push and turn on carpet, and battery collapses to roughly 9 minutes.

Second, the anti-tangle marketing is overstated for the main floor head. Despite Dyson's "detangling" claims, TechGearLab found 7-inch test hair strands still wrapped around the roller, and called it out as marketing overstatement. Vacuum Wars agreed that the main floor head traps some hair — but praised the hair screw handheld attachment as excellent for tangle-prone long hair. So the capability exists; it just lives on the right tool rather than the primary head.

Nerd Box

The green laser is not a gimmick

The V15's Fluffy Optic / Laser Slim Fluffy hardwood head projects a green laser across the floor at a low angle. Physically, low-angle raking light casts long shadows off fine dust that ambient room light hides, so particles you would normally miss become visible mid-clean. It is one of the few "feature" gimmicks that has a real optical basis, and it pairs with the piezo sensor that counts and sizes particles for the on-handle dust readout. The 9.0/10 hardwood score and 99% measured hardwood pickup suggest the whole system is doing real work, not just theater.

Pricing: Expect Variance

Do not anchor to one number. TechGearLab lists the V15 Detect at $750 (a figure still live on their Editors' Choice review as of our research). Vacuum Wars logged a $649.99 "current price as of May 18, 2026" for the Detect Plus (Yellow/Nickel) at Amazon. Bundle matters a lot here: the base Detect, Detect Plus and Detect Absolute carry different tool kits and filter configs, and Dyson runs frequent sales. Frame your budget as roughly $650–$750 and buy when a config with the tools you want dips toward the bottom of that band.

Bottom Line

The Dyson V15 Detect is the rare "old flagship" that is still one of the smartest cordless vacuums you can buy. Two independent labs that purchase their own units — TechGearLab and Vacuum Wars — both rank it near the very top of the category, and its measured cleaning performance on hardwood, carpet and pet hair beats category averages comfortably. Its flaws are real but narrow: the hold-down trigger and the ~9-minute Max-power window are ergonomic annoyances, and the main head's anti-tangle marketing oversells what the hair screw tool actually delivers. None of that undercuts how well it cleans. If you want the absolute best and can spend $1,050+, the Gen5detect is the upgrade; if you want most of that performance for $650–$750, this is the one to buy.
